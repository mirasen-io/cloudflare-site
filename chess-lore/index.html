Mirasen Chess Lore

Chess Lore is a workflow-first system for structured chess learning — designed to help turn training, study, and teaching material into clearer review, stronger retention, and more meaningful progress over time.

Overview

Workflow-first structured chess learning

Chess Lore is not centered on isolated puzzles or content fragments alone. The current direction is built around workflows: how material is created, assigned, reviewed, reinforced, and made useful over time.

Current direction

The current product direction starts with trainer workflows: helping trainers organize, assign, review, and reinforce learning more effectively.

Broader reach

The same learning logic also supports self-directed learners who want stronger structure, clearer review, and more durable progress from their own material.

Core learning model

From positions to reusable learning units

The current semantic foundation centers on motifs and context. Instead of treating positions only as isolated examples, Chess Lore aims to make them part of a richer learning structure that explains not only what happens, but why it matters here.

Motifs

The atomic tactical or positional patterns that give a position learning meaning.

Context

Structured metadata describing where and why a motif appears in a particular position.

Learning units

Positions and lines that can move beyond one-time explanation and become reusable material for review and reinforcement.

What it supports

Review, retention, and follow-up

The goal is not only to store material, but to support what happens after explanation: review, reinforcement, and progress visibility tied to real training activity.

This is especially important when the material comes from personal games, trainer assignments, annotated examples, and recurring mistakes — the kinds of learning material that often matter most but are hard to revisit well over time.

review retention follow-up progress visibility
  • Turn important material into ongoing learning
  • Support more structured reinforcement over time
  • Make progress more visible and more meaningful

Who it is for

Trainer-first, but not trainer-only

The current direction is trainer-first because trainer workflows create a strong foundation for assignment, review, and visible learning progress. At the same time, the broader learning model is also relevant to students studying more independently.

Trainers

Organize homework, reinforce important material, and gain clearer visibility into how learning develops over time.

Students

Revisit important material more effectively, retain more of what matters, and follow a clearer learning path through structured review.

Inside Mirasen

How Chess Lore fits the broader structure

Mirasen is the broader brand and ecosystem. Chess Lore is the current main product direction inside that work, while Chessboard is the open-source foundation layer that supports current and future tools.

This keeps the structure clear: one brand, one current main product direction, and one open-source foundation layer.
